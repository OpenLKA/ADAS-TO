  MODELING NOTES — Take Over Analysis (Section IV)

METHOD SUMMARY
----------------------------------------

(A) Binary outcome models use GEE logistic regression with
    exchangeable correlation structure, clustered by driver (dongle_id).
    Fallback: cluster-robust GLM if GEE fails to converge.
    Subset: lead-present clips only (TTC/DRAC further restricted to closing).
    Rationale: avoids right-censoring and heavy-tailed issues with continuous
    TTC/THW regression; threshold-event modeling is standard in traffic safety.

(B) Continuous outcome models use linear mixed-effects models (REML)
    with driver random intercept.
    ICC quantifies driver-level variance share.
    Stabilization time model restricted to uncensored observations.

(C) OEM vs OP comparison uses inverse probability weighting (IPW).
    Propensity covariates: ['pre_speed_mean_mps', 'pre_lead_present_rate', 'pre_min_drel_m', 'log_hz', 'pre_max_abs_steer_angle_deg']
    Overlap restriction: propensity in (0.1, 0.9)
    SMD balance threshold: 0.1
    Bootstrap CI (n=1000) for weighted treatment effects.

CAVEATS
----------------------------------------
- TTC/THW are only defined when a lead vehicle is detected;
  results are conditional on lead presence.
- Jerk and steer_rate are sampling-rate sensitive; qlog (10Hz)
  and rlog (100Hz) clips may produce systematically different values.
  See qlog vs rlog sensitivity in derived signals summary.
- Propensity weighting assumes no unmeasured confounders;
  self-selection into openpilot is likely correlated with
  unobserved driver characteristics.
- Stabilization time uses a right-censored definition at 5.0s;
  censored clips are excluded from LMM (reported as descriptive).
